Conservation Initiatives

India has numerous conservation initiatives aimed at preserving its rich biodiversity and natural resources.
Here's a list of some prominent ones:

1. Project Tiger :

Launched in 1973, it aims to conserve the population of Bengal tigers in their natural habitats, as well as to
increase their numbers through habitat preservation and protection.

    Owned By :

Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the National Tiger Conservation Authority (NTCA)
under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change.

    Roadmap :

  • Identify tiger reserves and core habitats for protection.
  • Implement measures to reduce human-tiger conflicts.
  • Enhance anti-poaching efforts and improve law enforcement.
  • Conduct scientific research and monitoring of tiger populations.
  • Collaborate with local communities for sustainable conservation efforts.
  • Increase public awareness and support for tiger conservation.

    • 2. Project Elephant :

    Started in 1992, this initiative focuses on the conservation of elephants and their habitats.
    It aims to address human-elephant conflicts, protection of corridors, and management
    of captive elephants.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the Project Elephant Division under
    the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change.

        Roadmap :

  • Identify elephant corridors and critical habitats for protection.
  • Implement measures to mitigate human-elephant conflicts.
  • Enhance habitat management and protection.
  • Monitor elephant populations and health status.
  • Implement captive elephant management guidelines.
  • Collaborate with states and local communities for conservation efforts.

    • 3. National Mission for Clean Ganga (Namami Gange) :

    Launched in 2014, this initiative aims to clean and rejuvenate the Ganges river, one of India's most
    sacred and polluted rivers, through various conservation measures and community involvement.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG)
    under the Ministry of Jal Shakti.

        Roadmap :

  • Implement sewage treatment plants and sewage infrastructure development.
  • Promote afforestation and wetland conservation along the Ganges basin.
  • Control industrial pollution and promote cleaner production technologies.
  • Implement riverfront development projects with ecological considerations.
  • Create public awareness and community involvement in river conservation.

    • 4. National Afforestation Programme (NAP) :

    Implemented by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, NAP focuses on
    increasing forest cover and promoting afforestation and reforestation activities
    across the country.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the Ministry of Environment, Forest
    and Climate Change, along with state forest departments.

        Roadmap :

  • Identify degraded forest lands for afforestation and reforestation.
  • Implement tree planting and habitat restoration projects.
  • Promote agroforestry and community-based forest management.
  • Enhance capacity building and technical support for afforestation efforts.
  • Monitor and evaluate afforestation outcomes for ecological benefits.

    • 5. Wildlife Protection Act :

    Enacted in 1972 and amended several times since then, this legislation provides legal protection
    to wildlife and their habitats, regulates hunting and trade of wildlife, and establishes
    protected areas such as national parks and sanctuaries.

        Owned By :

    Owned and enforced by the Government of India through the Ministry of Environment, Forest and
    Climate Change.

        Roadmap :

  • Enforce strict regulations against poaching, hunting, and trade of wildlife.
  • Establish protected areas such as national parks, sanctuaries, and biosphere reserves.
  • Conduct wildlife surveys and habitat assessments.
  • Implement conservation programs for endangered species.
  • Collaborate with local communities for wildlife conservation and habitat management.

    • 6. National Mission for Green India (GIM) :

    Launched in 2014, GIM aims to improve forest and tree cover, enhance ecosystem services, and
    increase carbon sequestration through afforestation and reforestation activities.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and
    Climate Change.

        Roadmap :

  • Increase forest cover and biodiversity through afforestation and reforestation.
  • Promote sustainable forest management practices.
  • Enhance carbon sequestration and ecosystem services.
  • Implement agroforestry and social forestry programs.
  • Involve local communities in forest conservation and livelihood improvement.

    • 7. National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) :

    Launched in 2019, NCAP aims to tackle air pollution in Indian cities by implementing various
    measures such as increasing monitoring, controlling emissions from industries and
    vehicles, and promoting cleaner technologies.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB)
    under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, along with state pollution
    control boards.

        Roadmap :

  • Monitor air quality and pollution levels in urban areas.
  • Implement pollution control measures for industries and vehicles.
  • Promote cleaner technologies and renewable energy sources.
  • Create public awareness and citizen participation in air quality management.
  • Conduct research and development for air pollution control technologies.

    • 8. Coral Reef Conservation Programme :

    India has various initiatives focused on the conservation of coral reefs, especially in regions like
    the Andaman and Nicobar Islands and the Gulf of Mannar. These programs aim to protect
    coral reefs from threats such as pollution, overfishing, and climate change.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by various agencies including the Ministry of
    Environment, Forest and Climate Change and the Ministry of Earth Sciences.

        Roadmap :

  • Conduct surveys and assessments of coral reef ecosystems.
  • Implement marine protected areas and marine spatial planning.
  • Monitor coral health and biodiversity.
  • Implement measures to mitigate coral bleaching and degradation.
  • Promote sustainable fishing practices and marine tourism.

    • 9. Save the Tiger Campaign :

    This campaign, led by various NGOs and wildlife conservation organizations, aims to raise
    awareness about the plight of tigers and garner public support for their conservation.

        Owned By :

    Owned and implemented by various NGOs and wildlife conservation organizations
    in collaboration with the Government of India.

        Roadmap :

  • Raise public awareness about tiger conservation issues.
  • Support anti-poaching efforts and wildlife law enforcement.
  • Implement community-based conservation projects.
  • Promote habitat protection and restoration.
  • Collaborate with government agencies and NGOs for tiger conservation initiatives.

    • 10. National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) :

    Launched in 2014, NMSA aims to promote sustainable agriculture practices,
    conserve natural resources, and enhance resilience to climate change among farmers.

        Owned By :

    Owned by the Government of India, implemented by the Department of Agriculture,
    Cooperation & Farmers Welfare under the Ministry of Agriculture &
    Farmers Welfare.

        Roadmap :

  • Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices.
  • Implement soil conservation and water management measures.
  • Support organic farming and integrated pest management.
  • Enhance agricultural productivity and rural livelihoods.
  • Provide technical assistance and capacity building for sustainable agriculture practices.
